Staying informed

We use several channels to share information about upcoming DS talks, schedule changes, and other news.

Calendar

The easiest way to keep track of talks is to subscribe to our online calendar. All confirmed talks are added with titles, speakers, dates, times, and connection details.

  • You can view the calendar in your browser.
  • You can also subscribe to it from most calendar applications (Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook, etc.) using the provided URL.

Open the DS calendar

Mailing list

We maintain a low-volume mailing list for announcements. Before each talk, we send out an email with the title, abstract, and connection information.

  • Typically, you will receive one email per talk, plus occasional general announcements.
  • You can unsubscribe at any time via a link at the bottom of every message.

Subscribe to the announcement list

Announcement blog and RSS

For those who prefer RSS or like to browse announcements in one place, we keep an announcement blog where each talk has its own post. This is a good way to scroll through past and upcoming talks.

  • Every talk is announced with title, speaker, abstract, and basic logistics.
  • If you use an RSS reader, you can subscribe to the blog’s RSS feed and receive new posts automatically.

Visit the announcement blog · RSS feed

Social updates

We also post brief announcements of new talks and other news on our social accounts. Follow us there if that is your preferred way to stay up to date.

  • Bluesky: @mycsplus.bsky.social

YouTube and RSS

Most talks are recorded and made available on our YouTube channel. If you subscribe there, you will be notified when new recordings are posted.

You can also follow the channel via an RSS feed, which is handy if you prefer to use a feed reader rather than the YouTube interface.
